The results paragraph in the paper is:

The mean initial population size was 3045 (95% credible intervals of 2720;3611) (figure 4, top left panel). The mean of the survival pre oil spill was 0.943 (95% credible intervals of 0.931;0.953) while the mean of the survival post oil spill was 0.818 (95% credible intervals of 0.695;0.909), leading to an average reduction in survival post-oil spill of 0.868 (95% credible intervals of 0.737;0.964). The average population trajectory under the presence of the oil spill and assuming the oil spill never happened are shown in Figure 4 (bottom panel). There were 28418 median LCY (95% percentile CI 6978-83760), the maximum proportional decrease was 44.1 % (95% percentile CI 15.5-72.7) and it took a median of 32 (95% percentile CI 18-56) years for 95 % recovery.
